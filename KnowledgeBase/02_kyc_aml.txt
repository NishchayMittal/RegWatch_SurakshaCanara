Department: KYC / AML
Regulator Relevance: RBI (primary), SEBI (secondary), MCA (tertiary)

HANDLES:
- Know Your Customer (KYC) policy updates and re-KYC drives
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) program maintenance and staff training
- Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) procedures
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR) filing with Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU-IND)
- Cash Transaction Reports (CTR) — transactions above Rs. 10 lakh
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) screening and monitoring
- Beneficial ownership identification for corporate accounts
- FATF compliance and correspondent banking due diligence
- Aadhaar-based eKYC and Video KYC (V-CIP) implementation
- PMLA (Prevention of Money Laundering Act) obligations
- Sanctions screening (UN, OFAC, EU lists)
- Risk categorisation of customers (Low / Medium / High)

DOES NOT HANDLE:
- Capital adequacy or liquidity ratios (→ Treasury & Investments)
- Loan underwriting or credit decisions (→ Credit Risk)
- Securities trading compliance (→ Retail Banking Compliance)
- Cybersecurity of KYC systems (→ IT & Cybersecurity)
- Employee background checks (→ HR & Conduct)

KEYWORDS:
KYC, AML, CFT, CDD, EDD, STR, CTR, FIU, PMLA, PEP, beneficial owner,
Aadhaar, eKYC, V-CIP, video KYC, sanctions, OFAC, FATF, money laundering,
terrorist financing, re-KYC, customer due diligence, risk categorisation,
periodic updation, OVD, officially valid document, CKYC, CKYCR

REAL REGULATORY REFERENCES:
- RBI Master Direction on KYC (RBI/2015-16/42, updated 2023) — the primary reference
- RBI Circular on Video based Customer Identification Process (RBI/2019-20/163)
- RBI Circular on Central KYC Records Registry (CKYCR) — DBR.AML.BC.No.19/14.01.001/2018-19
- PMLA Rules 2005 and subsequent amendments (Ministry of Finance)
- RBI Circular on Beneficial Ownership (DBR.AML.BC.No.44/14.01.001/2018-19)
- SEBI Master Circular on AML/CFT for intermediaries (SEBI/HO/MIRSD/DOP/P/CIR/2022/86)

SAMPLE MAP 1:
Circular Source: RBI
MAP Text: "All Regulated Entities (REs) shall ensure periodic updation of KYC for existing customers. High risk customers: every 2 years. Medium risk: every 8 years. Low risk: every 10 years. REs shall put in place a board-approved policy for periodic updation by [date] and submit compliance certificate to RBI."
Correct Department: KYC / AML
Confidence Signal: HIGH — 'KYC', 'periodic updation', 'high risk customers', 'regulated entities' are unambiguous KYC terms
Penalty if missed: Monetary penalty under Section 47 of PMLA — up to Rs. 1 lakh per day of default

SAMPLE MAP 2:
Circular Source: RBI
MAP Text: "Banks shall file Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with FIU-IND within 7 days of arriving at a conclusion that a transaction is suspicious. The Principal Officer designated under PMLA shall be responsible for timely filing. Banks shall maintain records of all STRs filed for a period of 5 years."
Correct Department: KYC / AML
Confidence Signal: HIGH — 'STR', 'FIU-IND', 'Principal Officer', 'PMLA' are definitive AML markers
Penalty if missed: Criminal prosecution of Principal Officer under PMLA Section 12A; bank penalties up to Rs. 1 crore
