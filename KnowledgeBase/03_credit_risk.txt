Department: Credit Risk
Regulator Relevance: RBI (primary)

HANDLES:
- Non-Performing Asset (NPA) classification and provisioning norms
- Income recognition and asset classification (IRAC norms)
- Stressed asset resolution — IBC, SARFAESI, OTS, restructuring
- Expected Credit Loss (ECL) provisioning framework (Ind AS 109)
- Large Exposure Framework (LEF) — single borrower and group exposure limits
- Sectoral exposure limits and concentration risk
- Credit risk rating and internal rating-based (IRB) approach
- Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratios for secured lending
- Priority sector lending (PSL) compliance and targets
- Risk-weighted assets (RWA) computation for credit risk
- MSME restructuring and emergency credit lines
- Resolution plans under RBI's Prudential Framework for Stressed Assets

DOES NOT HANDLE:
- CRR/SLR or liquidity ratios (→ Treasury & Investments)
- KYC or customer onboarding decisions (→ KYC/AML)
- Trading or market risk in investment portfolio (→ Treasury & Investments)
- Legal enforcement of SARFAESI actions (→ Legal & Regulatory Affairs)
- Fraud investigation in loan accounts (→ Audit & Inspection)

SAMPLE MAP 1:
Circular Source: RBI
MAP Text: "All scheduled commercial banks shall classify accounts as Special Mention Account (SMA) on the basis of principal or interest payment overdue: SMA-0 (1-30 days), SMA-1 (31-60 days), SMA-2 (61-90 days). Banks shall report SMA status of all borrowers with aggregate exposure of Rs. 5 crore and above to CRILC on a weekly basis."
Correct Department: Credit Risk
Confidence Signal: HIGH — 'SMA', 'NPA classification', 'CRILC reporting', 'overdue' are unambiguous credit risk terms
Penalty if missed: Regulatory action under RBI Supervisory Framework; delayed CRILC reporting attracts penalty

SAMPLE MAP 2:
Circular Source: RBI
MAP Text: "Banks shall maintain provisions for Non-Performing Assets as follows: Sub-standard assets — 15% (secured), 25% (unsecured). Doubtful assets (up to 1 year) — 25%. Doubtful assets (1-3 years) — 40%. Doubtful assets (above 3 years) — 100%. Loss assets — 100%. Banks shall compute and report provision coverage ratio (PCR) quarterly."
Correct Department: Credit Risk
Confidence Signal: HIGH — 'NPA provisioning', 'substandard', 'doubtful', 'loss assets', 'PCR' are core credit risk terms
Penalty if missed: Underprovisioning triggers RBI corrective action; misstatement of financials
